6 Best Places for Solo Travel in the Philippines


“You’ll never know if you don’t go”

Thinking of traveling solo and exploring new places on your own? Don’t let fear keep you from seeing the world out there. It can be nerve-wracking from deciding whether to go, planning and making that final decision to hop on that plane and go.

If tropical islands, mind-blowing landscapes, and unending adventure are in your bucket list for a holiday trip, then you have found a gem of a destination with the Philippines.

From paradise-like beaches and idyllic islands with turquoise waters, to the world’s most perfect cone volcano and the smallest primate, to a surfing mecca and century-old Spanish fortresses — there are unlimited surprises waiting for every traveler who will visit the Philippines.

Read on to get a glimpse of which top tourist spots you should definitely not miss on this trip of a lifetime!



1. Baguio

The hours-long land travel to Baguio via bus is what makes your solo trip adventurous and much more exciting. This will allow you to do sightseeing and savor the view along the way. Baguio is like a friendly city that’s always warm and welcoming, despite the cold weather. People travel to Baguio for a cooler weather, especially during summer. More than that, the City of Pines also has a rich cultural heritage that can be seen in Baguio tourist spots like museums of local artists, arts and craft shops as well as artsy restaurants found in the area.



2. La Union

When it comes to quick solo travel near Manila, La Union is the go-to place for friends coming from the city, but that doesn’t mean that you can't travel here solo.

One of the top reasons why it’s best to go here is its accessibility to/from Manila. In just a few hours, you can already reach the town proper of San Juan, La Union. Known for its laidback and relaxing surf beach vibe, La Union is a perfect quick escape if you want to destress and recharge without booking a flight. When traveling solo, it’s good to try things you’ve never done before and push yourself to the limit. So, if you haven’t tried surfing and have been wanting to ride the waves, La Union is a good place to start. It has one of the most beginner-friendly spots to learn.

Apart from surfing, there are some other unique activities you can do here like hiking the Tangadan Falls, cliff jumping, beach bumming and going on a food trip around town. At night, you can choose to grab a bottle or two at Tipple and Brew or mingle with fellow travelers and dance the night away at Flotsam and Jetsam.



3. Coron

Another Palawan destination, Coron is the type of place that offers you a lot of things to do with so little time. If you’re traveling solo, plan your itinerary based on the main reason for your visit.

Would it be to go on an adventure? To discover the history of the place and immerse in the culture? Or maybe, to just relax, reflect and give your mind and body a break they deserve.

There are a lot of Coron tourist spots and activities in store for every type of traveler. But the main attractions are Coron island-hopping tours that will take you to pristine beaches, jaw-dropping lagoons and gorgeous islands like Kayangan Lake.

It may not be a party island like the more popular Boracay, but it houses some good hang out places where you can socialize and meet new people from all over the world. Watching the sunset over a bottle of beer and chatting with newfound friends do sound like a good idea.

If you’re traveling solo for the first time, it’s recommended that you choose a place where it’s safe and with friendly locals to help you navigate around. Coron fits the bill, plus it has hostels and hotels that are budget-friendly for backpackers and solo travelers. There are also cheap restaurants where you can dine and enjoy the local cuisine of Palawan.

5. Batanes

There’s a reason why Batanes is in every traveler’s bucket list. Everywhere you look is a scenic sight to behold. The tourist spots in Batanes like its magnificent seascapes and landscapes will make you sigh in admiration and will leave you in awe as you breathe it all in.

Imagine yourself standing at the top of the mountain with the breathtaking view of the Pacific Ocean in front of you, surrounded by the verdant, rolling hills, with majestic lighthouses. It’s almost perfect you can’t even believe that a place like this exists.

Batanes is one of those places that will make you realize that traveling solo is the best decision you’ve ever made because life is meant to be lived seeing this kind of masterpiece – with or without a group of friends or a significant someone.

Undeniably, Batanes is a serene place that is perfect for anyone who would want to embark on an unforgettable solo adventure. Although the airfare to Basco, Batanes might be a bit pricey compared to other tourist destinations in the Philippines, it’s not impossible to visit here on a budget. There are homestays that are best suited for solo travelers.

Likewise, the Ivatans are known for being honest and kind so rest assured that you won’t have difficulty relating with the locals. In fact, most of the accomodations in Batanes here have the honesty store concept.



6. Siargao

Siargao has an inexplicable charm and vibe that makes it an ideal destination for solo travelers. The island can make you feel like home in no time as it exudes an unintimidating aura that gives you the feeling of belongingness right away.

You will never really feel alone in Siargao as long as you’re open to meeting new people and potential friends. The island is a popular destination for solo travelers, backpackers, local and international tourists who visit to see the magic of this beautiful island.

Given the diversity of people in Siargao, it wouldn’t be hard to settle and get the hang of it even if you’re traveling alone. Surfers and non-surfers alike will surely enjoy the variety of activities and fun things to do in Siargao.You can walk by the beach, do sightseeing tours in Cloud 9 or do a bit of a food trip session with the wide range of good restaurants around. If you get tired of exploring the place on your own, you can join group tours like island-hopping and land exploration.


It’s a good way to socialize with different people from other places in and out of the Philippines and share travel stories or tips which can help you make the most out of your solo adventures.

Safety Tips when Traveling Solo in the Philippines

It’s freeing and exciting to go on a solo adventure and accept the challenge of stepping into the unknown. It brings out your independence and confidence.

It also makes you become more aware of your surroundings to keep you safe wherever you are. Here are some safety travel tips to keep in mind:

Stay connected

While you don’t have to be online all the time, it’s still better to update your family and friends about your itineraries and whereabouts. Send them a quick message that you’ve arrived safely in your destination or if there’s any change of plans on your end.

Bring emergency money

Some tourist spots have limited ATM machines and might not be available at all times. It’s better to bring enough pocket money to make sure that you're covered for the entire trip or cover additional costs that you didn't plan for.

Talk to the locals

Traveling alone doesn’t mean you have to isolate yourself for the whole duration of the trip. This is even the best time to broaden your knowledge and immerse yourself in the place’s culture. Engaging with the locals for some tips and recommendations is a good way to know where most people hang out and the safest spots for solo travelers like you.

Research in advance

Apart from the things to do and attractions to see, it would also be good to research current news or events in the place you’re heading to. This will give you an idea of what to expect and how to prepare for any unfortunate events that may arise while you’re there. Read our ultimate guides on Philippine destinations to learn more.

Familiarize yourself with the transportation system or options

Getting lost is part of the adventure but it’s still advisable to know the best way to explore the area to save you from wasting time and money.

Trust your instinct

If something feels off, listen to your gut. Be open to meeting new people and going on the unexplored paths but be extra alert and aware to make sure that you won’t be taken advantage of. Traveling solo will teach you how to be self-reliant as you conquer your fears and enjoy life to the fullest on your own.
